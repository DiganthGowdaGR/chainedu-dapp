# Introduction to Blockchain Architecture

A blockchain is a decentralized, distributed, and oftentimes public, digital ledger consisting of records called blocks that are used to record transactions across many computers so that any involved block cannot be altered retroactively.

## Core Concepts
1. Decentralization: No central authority controls the network.
2. Cryptography: Uses SHA-256 hashing to secure the blocks.
3. Consensus: Mechanisms like Proof of Work or Proof of Stake.

## Smart Contracts
Self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement directly written into lines of code.
